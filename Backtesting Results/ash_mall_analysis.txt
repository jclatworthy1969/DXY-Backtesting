SECTION 4: ENTRY PATTERN IDENTIFICATION
================================================================================

From notes analysis, Ash uses at least FOUR candle confirmation patterns:

1. REJECTION / PIN BAR
   - Notes: "rejection trade" (×2), "rejection going with the HTF", "Pin bar entry"
   - Description: Price touches level, wick rejects it, candle closes back in
     opposite direction. Upper/lower wick > body, closing away from level.
   - SAME AS BRICE'S: "Bounce/rejection" at level
   - DIFFERENT FROM OUR ALGO: We look for big-body candles; rejection trades
     have SMALL bodies and LARGE wicks — the opposite of our trigger candle.

2. ENGULFING CANDLE
   - Notes: "Ebgulfing entry"
   - Description: Current candle's body completely covers prior candle's body
   - SAME AS BRICE'S: Engulfing at level is his primary confirmation
   - SAME AS OUR ALGO: We detect large-body candles, but at the zone rather
     than at the precise level after retest.

3. THREE-BAR PATTERN (3-bar reversal)
   - Notes: "3 bar reversal short with 4H TF"
   - Description: Bar 1 sets direction, bar 2 pullback, bar 3 confirms reversal
   - SAME AS BRICE'S: He uses 3-bar explicitly for both reversals and continuations

4. GENERAL REVERSAL ("reversal with HTF 1H & 4H aligns with BBand")
   - Combined: HTF alignment on both 1H AND 4H, plus BBand confirmation
   - Suggests he uses BB expansion direction as the final confirmation gate

CRITICAL INSIGHT: Ash uses REJECTION (pin bar) as a primary entry pattern.
This is candle-pattern confirmation DISTINCT from our big-candle trigger.
A rejection pin bar at the session opening level is structurally the OPPOSITE
of what our algo fires on — we look for large momentum candles, he looks for
wicks that test and reject a level. Both traders use multiple candle patterns
but BOTH require the candle to occur AT the level, not approaching it.

SECTION 10: KEY NEW INSIGHTS FROM ASH vs BRICE
================================================================================

10.1 REJECTION / PIN BAR AS PRIMARY ENTRY PATTERN (NEW vs Brice analysis)
    Ash explicitly labels several trades as "rejection trade" — this means a
    pin bar / wick rejection at the level, NOT an engulfing candle.
    Brice focused on engulfing and 3-bar; Ash adds rejection/pin bar.
    IMPLICATION: Our algo should detect BOTH large-body candles (engulfing)
    AND long-wick reversal bars (rejection/pin bar) at the opening level.
    A rejection bar has a small body but a large wick pointing away from the
    level — body/range ratio < 0.3 with wick on level side > 60% of range.

10.2 "MITIGATED LEVELS" CONCEPT (NEW vs Brice analysis)
    Ash uses "mitigated level" terminology — a price level that has been
    previously tested/visited by price. This is a broader concept than just
    the session opening: any previously-respected S/R level qualifies.
    This extends beyond Brice's session open to include historical pivot points.
    IMPLICATION: The session opening level is the PRIMARY level (cleanest,
    most fresh), but previously mitigated S/R levels are also valid if they
    haven't been revisited recently.

10.3 BOTH 1H AND 4H BBBAND REQUIRED (CONFIRMATION of Brice)
    Ash's note: "Reversal with HTF 1H & 4H aligns with BBand"
    This explicitly states: BOTH timeframes, BOTH aligned, Bollinger Band
    confirmation. Not just one timeframe, not just price vs MA — the BB
    regime must agree on both 1H and 4H.
    IMPLICATION: Check BB expansion/direction on BOTH 1H and 4H before entry.
    If 1H BBs are expanding one way but 4H BBs are flat/opposite → skip.

10.4 HTF ALIGNMENT IS NON-NEGOTIABLE FOR ATTR TRADES
    Both traders' ATTR losses explicitly mention "against HTF":
    - Ash: "Open Gap Attraction against the HTF some lost but break even"
    - Brice: Multiple losses from "so much momentum" (= against HTF)
    This is the clearest and most consistent rule across both datasets:
    NEVER take an attraction trade when it conflicts with the HTF trend.
    Attraction trades are only valid when HTF is flat/neutral or supporting.

10.5 TRADE FREQUENCY COMPARISON
    Ash post Aug-23: ~10.6 trades/month (177 over 16.6 months)
    Brice post Aug-23: ~11.0 trades/month (181 over 16.4 months)
    Our algo REV: ~6.2 trades/month
    Our algo ATTR: ~8.6 trades/month
    Total our algo: ~14.8 trades/month
    Both real traders take ~10-11 trades/month with ~82% WR.
    Our algo takes ~50% more per month with ~53% WR.
    We are generating too many low-quality signals. Stricter criteria would
