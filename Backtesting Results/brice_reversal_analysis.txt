3.1 CANDLE PATTERNS (explicit in notes)
----------------------------------------
a) Engulfing candle at key level
   - Required as ENTRY confirmation, NOT as the signal itself
   - [2023-08-17 LOSS]: "just saw large engulging" without waiting for retest = fail
   - [2024-11-20 WIN]:  "previous demand zone on engulfing" = works
   - [2024-10-15 WIN]:  "engulfing candle on second rejection" in W-pattern = works
   RULE IMPLICATION: Engulfing alone is not enough. It must be AT a structural level
   AND preceded by a valid retest of that level.

b) Three-bar / 3-bar entry
   - [2023-10-10]: "following trend on 3bar"
   - [2024-12-12]: "three bar entry with long bias off opening"
   RULE IMPLICATION: 3-bar pattern (presumably: bar 1 sets high/low, bar 2 pulls back,
   bar 3 enters in trend direction) is used for both reversals and continuations.

c) W-pattern / double bottom/top
   - [2024-10-15]: first touch failed, second touch + engulfing = entry
   RULE IMPLICATION: Do not enter on first test of a level. Wait for second test
   with engulfing confirmation = higher-probability entry.

3.2 ZONE/LEVEL REFERENCES (explicit in notes)
----------------------------------------------
a) DXY Opening Level / "Pristine Opening"
   - Most frequently referenced level
   - [2024-11-27]: "pristine opening below finally understood" — the day's or prior
     session's opening price acted as support
   - [2024-10-31]: "DXY open in helping with consolidation areas"
   - [2024-12-12]: "long bias off opening"
   - [2024-03-21]: "perfect retest of opening"
   RULE IMPLICATION: The DXY daily open (or London session open) is the PRIMARY
   support/resistance level. "Pristine" = a clean, untested opening level.

b) 4-Hour Moving Average (as structural zone)
   - [2024-10-07 LOSS]: "room to return to MA on 4hr" — tried to fade trend to MA = fail
   - [2024-08-08]: "4hr ma flat" = caution, not pristine
   - [2024-08-28 LOSS]: "HTF was above MA on both" — consolidation around MA = ambiguous
   RULE IMPLICATION: 4H MA is used as a support/resistance level, but ONLY when
   price has momentum away from it and is clearly above/below (not flat/consolidating).

c) Previous Demand Zone
   - [2024-11-20]: "previous demand zone on engulfing"
   RULE IMPLICATION: Historical demand/supply zones from prior price action are valid
   level references, especially when engulfing confirmation occurs at the zone.

d) Fibonacci Retracement Levels
   - [2024-10-29, 2024-10-31]: Fibs added to toolkit in Oct 2024
   - Used specifically in CONSOLIDATION to identify breakout direction
   - Also used with trend lines for structure mapping

3.3 HTF CONTEXT AND BIAS (explicit in notes)
--------------------------------------------
a) 4H price vs MA
   - "HTF was above MA on both [timeframes]" = bullish bias
   - 4H MA flat = neutral/no-trade signal
   - 4H MA turning over = trend change signal
   RULE: Both 1H and 4H must have price clearly on same side of MA for strong bias.

b) 1H Bollinger Bands
   - "1hr bands expanding MA turning over" [2024-10-18] = trend change confirmation
   - "took continuation with expanding bands" [2024-09-19 LOSS] — bands alone insufficient
   - "pristine opening below" combined with bands [2024-11-27 WIN]
   RULE: 1H Bollinger bands expanding = momentum confirmation, but requires level confluence.
   Bands expanding in direction of trade + price at structural level = valid entry.
   Bands expanding alone (without level reference) = insufficient for reversal entry.

c) HTF trend direction
   - "with htf trend" [2024-10-15]
   - "Going against previous trend but 1hr bands expanding MA turning over" [2024-10-18]
   RULE: Preferred trades are WITH HTF trend. Counter-trend entries require bands +
   MA turn as additional evidence of genuine trend change.

CRITERION-BY-CRITERION COMPARISON
-----------------------------------

1. ENTRY SIGNAL
   Our algo : Big candle (body >= 200 pts, range >= 300 pts) fires the signal
   Brice    : Engulfing candle at the opening level, OR 3-bar pattern, OR W-pattern
              (second rejection with engulfing at same level)
   Gap      : Our big-candle trigger fires on momentum INTO a level, not ON the level.
              Brice waits for the reversal confirmation AFTER price reaches the level.
              He explicitly notes [2023-08-17] that taking the big candle BEFORE the
              retest confirmation is a mistake.

2. ZONE / LEVEL REFERENCE
   Our algo : Supply/demand zone library, price within 500 pts
   Brice    : Primarily uses the DXY SESSION OPENING LEVEL (London or daily open)
              Also uses 4H MA and historical demand zones as secondary levels.
   Gap      : Our zones are static supply/demand boxes. His primary reference is the
              DAILY/LONDON OPEN — a dynamic, session-specific level. The "pristine
              opening" concept means an untouched opening level is the strongest setup.
              His entries appear to be within 0-10 pips (0-100 pts) of the opening,
              far tighter than our 500-pt zone proximity filter.

3. HTF FILTER
   Our algo : 1H + 4H EMA20 vs EMA50 simple crossover
   Brice    : 4H price above/below MA + 1H Bollinger bands (direction + expansion)
              + MA slope (turning over = trend change)
   Gap      : Our EMA cross is binary. Brice's band system distinguishes between:
              - Trending market (bands expanding, MA sloping) = trade continuation
              - Consolidating market (bands flat, MA flat) = do NOT trade
              - Trend change (bands starting to expand in new direction, MA turning) = valid
              His flat-MA caution flag [2024-08-08: "4hr ma flat = not pristine"] directly
              filters out consolidation trades that our algo takes blindly.

4. ENTRY CONFIRMATION / CANDLE PATTERN
   Our algo : No bar-by-bar confirmation — big candle IS the entry trigger
   Brice    : REQUIRES retest confirmation after level touch:
              a) Engulfing at the level (body closes convincingly)
              b) 3-bar entry (pullback-confirm pattern)
              c) Second rejection (W-pattern — first touch fails, second confirms)
              He explicitly loses on [2023-08-17] for skipping this confirmation step.
   Gap      : This is likely the SINGLE BIGGEST contributor to the WR gap. Our algo
              can fire on a big candle that merely APPROACHES a zone. Brice waits for
              price to ARRIVE at the opening level, PULL BACK OR CONSOLIDATE there,
              then confirm with an engulfing or 3-bar pattern.

SECTION 6: MOST LIKELY REASONS FOR THE 30-POINT WR GAP (83.4% vs 53.2%)
================================================================================

Ranked by estimated impact on the WR gap:

REASON 1: ENTRY CONFIRMATION FILTER (estimated impact: ~10-15 percentage points)
  Our algo fires on the big candle itself. Brice waits for:
    - Price to reach the opening level
    - A confirming engulfing/3-bar/W-pattern on that level
  Our algo is entering ON momentum candles that may overshoot and reverse. Brice
  enters AFTER the momentum has completed and price is consolidating or bouncing
  at the level. The extra confirmation step filters out overshoot entries.

  Evidence: [2023-08-17] Brice explicitly loses when he skips this step. His note
  makes clear this is a RULE he violates, not a preference.

REASON 2: ZONE REFERENCE — OPENING LEVEL vs SUPPLY/DEMAND ZONES (~8-12 pts)
  Brice's primary reference is the DXY SESSION OPEN — a precise, session-specific
  price. Our algo uses static supply/demand boxes that are wider and more subjective.
  The opening level provides a single unambiguous reference price that the market
  regularly respects. Our zones may be misaligned or too wide.

  Evidence: "pristine opening", "perfect retest of opening", "long bias off opening"
  appear across multiple win trades. The opening level is a consistent anchor.

REASON 3: CONSOLIDATION / SIDEWAYS MARKET FILTER (~6-8 pts)
  Our algo fires in consolidating conditions. Brice explicitly skips them.
  In sideways markets, reversals are low-probability because there is no clear
  structural support for a directional move. Bollinger band width provides a
  mechanical way to detect these conditions.

  Evidence: [2024-07-25 LOSS], [2024-08-28 LOSS]. Both losses explicitly flagged
  as consolidation entries.

REASON 4: NEWS EVENT FILTER (~4-6 pts)
  Our algo takes trades right through NFP, CPI, ISM, etc. These events cause
  outside-normal-range moves that stop positions with no technical justification.
  Brice avoids or exits before scheduled events.

  Evidence: [2023-10-26 LOSS], [2024-04-04 LOSS], [2024-07-11] "risky!"

REASON 5: BOLLINGER BAND REGIME FILTER vs EMA CROSS (~3-5 pts)
  Bollinger bands provide regime information that simple EMA crossovers miss:
  - Flat/narrow bands = consolidation = skip
  - Expanding bands in trend direction = valid entry
  - MA turning + bands starting to expand = trend change valid entry
  EMA crossovers can give the "right" direction in a flat market with no momentum.
  Bands require actual price movement to expand, acting as a momentum gate.

REASON 6: STOP PLACEMENT STYLE (~2-4 pts)
  Our tight 20-bar swing pivot stops likely get hit by noise on otherwise-valid
  setups. Brice uses wider structural stops (~2600 pts median). This may increase
  the number of trades that survive initial volatility and reach target.
  The wider stop does NOT hurt his WR — it appears to help it.

REASON 7: LATE-DAY / CONFIDENCE FILTER (~2-3 pts)
  Brice skips trades when:
    - "Wasn't confident in the direction" [2024-07-22]
    - "Late in the day" (multiple mentions)
  Our algo has no such filter. It fires at any time within 07:30-12:00 UTC
  regardless of how clean the setup looks contextually.

SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDED ALGO IMPROVEMENTS
------------------------------------------
Priority 1 (HIGH IMPACT): Replace big-candle trigger with opening-level proximity
  + waiting for confirming bar (engulfing or 3-bar) after level touch.
  Rule: entry only when price is within X pts of session open AND next bar confirms
  direction (close past open of prior bar in trade direction).

Priority 2 (HIGH IMPACT): Use session opening price (London open, ~07:00 UTC) as
  primary level reference instead of or in addition to static supply/demand zones.

Priority 3 (MEDIUM IMPACT): Add Bollinger band width filter. Do not fire when 1H
  BB width < threshold (flat/consolidating market). Only fire when bands are
  expanding in the direction of the trade.
